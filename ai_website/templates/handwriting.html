What is MNIST?

MNIST is a benchmark dataset of 70,000 handwritten digits (0–9), each stored as a 28×28 grayscale image. It has been used to train and test machine learning models since 1998 and remains one of the most studied datasets in AI.

The model here achieved 97.9% accuracy by learning patterns across 120,000 augmented training samples.

Supervised Learning Classification Image Processing

How It Works

1 Draw: Your sketch is captured on a 280×280 canvas and scaled to 28×28 pixels.
2 Flatten: The 28×28 image becomes a vector of 784 pixel brightness values (0–255).
3 Forward pass: Values travel through two hidden layers of 256 neurons each, with ReLU activations.
4 Softmax: The output layer produces 10 probabilities — one per digit — that sum to 100%.
5 Predict: The digit with the highest probability is returned as the prediction.
Input (784) → Hidden (256) → Hidden (256) → Output (10)

Key Takeaways

Training vs. inference — The model was trained once on 120K images; now it just runs a fast forward pass on your drawing.
Confidence matters — A 99% confident prediction is very different from a 55% one. Always look at the top-3 scores.
Data augmentation — The training set was expanded with small rotations and shifts so the model handles messy handwriting better.
Softmax outputs — The 10 output probabilities always sum to 1, making them directly interpretable as confidence percentages.
