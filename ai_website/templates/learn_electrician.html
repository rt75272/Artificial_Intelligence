Electrician Apprenticeship Topics

Learn the fundamentals of electrical work and theory

Ohm's Law

Understand the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance (V = I × R). It's the foundation of all electrical theory.

Series vs. Parallel Circuits

Learn how components are wired together. In series, current is constant; in parallel, voltage is constant.

Electrical Safety

Master lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures, proper PPE usage, and safe working practices to prevent shock and arc flash.

National Electrical Code (NEC)

Navigate the NEC rulebook to ensure all installations meet legal safety standards and pass inspection.

AC/DC Theory

Discover the difference between Alternating Current (used in homes) and Direct Current (used in batteries/electronics).

Tools & Materials

Identify common hand tools, wire types (THHN, Romex), conduit (EMT, PVC), and how to properly use a multimeter.

Transformers

Learn how electromagnetic induction allows us to step AC voltages up for transmission and down for safe residential use.

Wire Sizing & Ampacity

Understand the American Wire Gauge (AWG) system and how to select the right wire size and breaker for a given load.

Motors & Generators

Explore the principles behind converting electrical energy into mechanical energy, and vice-versa.

Grounding & Bonding

Discover how proper grounding and bonding protect people and equipment from dangerous fault currents.
